Overview

Purpose

To establish an official site for our target customers to access information about the school's organization, curriculum, facilities & extra-curricular activities offered; view general announcements, FAQs, and location & contact information, especially for parents and/or guardians who desire a credible source of information about the school.

To direct traffic to our social media platforms where our main advertising campaign will be carried out.

To help communicate and support the school's overall brand i.e., "A school that forsters curiosity and promotes discovery of knowledge by learners through application of the scientific method in all fields of learning".

Audience

Our primary target customer group is households comprised of middle-income, open-minded parents/guardians between the ages of 25 to 50 years, where at least one parent/guardian has full-time employment and leads a busy life but still values quality education and appreciates the importance of both cognitive and non-cognitive skills (such as grit & perseverence) towards the success of their children. Such parents should be active and willing to devote additional resources (information, time etc.) towards their children's learning. They should also show a desire to form a community with other parents/guardians to continuously improve the school for the benefit of the learners.

Home page

Knowledge is Power, but Truth is Freedom

Hera School of Gifted Children offers a science-based curriculum that equips learners with the necessary skills to seek the truth and apply the knowledge they discover to change the world for the better.

Our Philosophy

In an age where truth is 'relative' and, if the studies are to be believed, where a lie travels six times faster than the truth, we here at Hera School of Gifted Children believe in absolute truths. Universal laws that govern not just our lives, but everything within the known universe. We believe in seeking these principles of truth, in understanding ourselves and each other and how and why the universe works. We believe that knowing the truth will indeed set us free as individuals and as a species.

"A man of science rises ever, in seeking truth; and if he never finds it in its wholeness, he discovers nevertheless very significant fragments; and these fragments of universal truth are precisely what constitutes science." ― Claude Bernard

"Without seeking, truth cannot be known at all. It can neither be declared from pulpits, nor set down in articles, nor in any wise prepared and sold in packages ready for use. Truth must be ground for every man by itself out of it such, with such help as he can get, indeed, but not without stern labor of his own." ― John Ruskin

Learning Approach

How We Teach, How We Learn: Discovering truths about our universe

Admittedly, there is a lot we do not understand about everything but our spirit of learning is driven by knowing what to do when you don't know what to do. For us that means first, accepting what you don't know and second, applying the scientific method of observation (measuring), prediction modelling, experimentation, observation (measuring) and objective analysis of the model repeating until the truth reveals itself. This is often an exercise of faith and requires humility, objectivity, hard work, perseverance, determination and grit. All values that we hope to cultivate in our learners along with a growth mindset. The belief that anyone can improve their intelligence, skills and talents through practice (iteration), perseverance, embracing challenges and viewing failure and positive criticism as an opportunity to learn and grow.

We believe that learning never ends and we encourage our learners to apply the same approach in all aspects of their lives be it in taking a free-kick, to studying the immune response of tomato plant to playing the guitar, to understanding what happens to your brain when you spend too much time on social media, to writing code to determine the fastest route for an ambulance to take based on current traffic.

We encourage our learners to be creative and curious, that is, to connect what they already know, to formulate hypotheses, and to actively test what they have learned in a safe way so as to promote long-term understanding. We We believe in sharing knowledge and we express this by encouraging our learners to teach one another, their friends, families and the world what they discover in their quest to understand these universal truths.

“The problem, as Randolph has realized, is that the best way for a young person to build character is for him to attempt something where there is a real and serious possibility of failure. In a high-risk endeavour, whether it's in business or athletics or the arts, you are more likely to experience colossal defeat than in a low-risk one—but you're also more likely to achieve real and original success. “The idea of building grit and building self-control is that you get that through failure,” Randolph explained.” ― Paul Tough, How Children Succeed: Grit, Curiosity, and the Hidden Power of Character
